About our Data

The project is built on four integrated components — county-level choropleths, an interactive charging map, a narrative layer, and a unified public-facing website — each matched to a specific data layer and task.
Choropleth maps in ArcGIS Online show the geographic distribution of EV adoption and the pacing gap. PEV population counts by county and year, drawn from the California Energy Commission's Light-Duty Vehicle Population dashboard (CEC, Zero Emission Vehicle and Infrastructure Statistics), are joined to ArcGIS Online county boundaries by FIPS code. PEV is defined as battery-electric plus plug-in hybrid vehicles; fuel-cell vehicles are excluded, since they depend on hydrogen stations rather than chargers. Regional share is computed as a population-weighted ratio — total PEVs over total light-duty vehicles across the six counties — rather than a simple county average, which would overstate it (≈7.97%) by weighting every county equally regardless of fleet size. The choropleths surface the project's first finding: a 2025 PEV population of 1,259,673 against a light-duty fleet of 16,140,791 — a weighted market share of 7.80%, with Orange County highest at 10.46% and San Bernardino lowest at 4.94%.
Data handling is bounded by the AB 2127 light-duty public-port scope. The CEC's expanded ZEV suite includes hydrogen refueling, medium- and heavy-duty, and school bus charging datasets; these were set aside as outside scope, along with private and workplace charging. The AB 2127 Second Assessment targets (CEC, March 2024) supply the benchmark throughout.
