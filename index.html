Background

Southern California is one of the largest electric vehicle markets in the United States, but charging infrastructure may not be growing evenly with EV adoption. As California moves toward its 2035 zero-emission vehicle sales target, public charging access is becoming an important transportation, infrastructure, and equity issue. Some areas may have high EV adoption but limited public charging capacity, while other areas may have stronger charger coverage.
The study will focus on six Southern California counties: Los Angeles, Orange, San Diego, Riverside, San Bernardino, and Ventura. The project will analyze EV registration patterns, public charging station locations, charger types, and charger-to-EV ratios using county and ZIP-code level data from approximately 2018-2025.
